1. Introduction to Our Copyright Policy

2. Educational Fair Use and Hosted Content

A significant portion of the materials referenced or hosted on MathHub Pro—such as NCERT textbooks, syllabus outlines, and Previous Year Question (PYQ) papers from the Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE)—are distributed under the principles of educational fair use and public domain access. These materials are originally produced by government educational bodies intended for free public consumption to aid in student preparation. MathHub Pro does not claim ownership of these government-produced academic texts. Our platform merely acts as an organized directory and educational hub to facilitate easier, high-speed access for students who require these materials for their academic advancement.

However, if any third-party publisher, educational entity, or copyright owner believes that their original, proprietary work has been copied, indexed, or uploaded to MathHub Pro in a way that constitutes copyright infringement, we are fully committed to resolving the issue immediately upon receiving a valid formal notification.

3. Submitting a DMCA Takedown Notice

If you are a copyright owner or an authorized agent thereof, and you believe that any content hosted on or linked to by MathHub Pro infringes upon your copyrights, you may submit a notification pursuant to the DMCA by providing our designated Copyright Agent with the following information in writing:

  • Physical or Electronic Signature: A physical or electronic signature of a person authorized to act on behalf of the owner of an exclusive right that is allegedly infringed.
  • Identification of the Copyrighted Work: Detailed identification of the copyrighted work claimed to have been infringed, or, if multiple copyrighted works on a single online site are covered by a single notification, a representative list of such works.
  • Identification of the Infringing Material: Clear identification of the material that is claimed to be infringing or to be the subject of infringing activity and that is to be removed or access to which is to be disabled. You must include the exact URL(s) on MathHub Pro where the material can be found.
  • Contact Information: Information reasonably sufficient to permit us to contact the complaining party, such as an address, telephone number, and an electronic mail (email) address.
  • Good Faith Statement: A statement that the complaining party has a good faith belief that use of the material in the manner complained of is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
  • Statement of Accuracy: A statement that the information in the notification is accurate, and under penalty of perjury, that the complaining party is authorized to act on behalf of the owner of an exclusive right that is allegedly infringed.

Please send your complete takedown notice directly to our administrative team at: mathhubpro@gmail.com. Please include "DMCA Takedown Request" in the subject line of your email to ensure expedited processing.

4. Review and Removal Process

Upon receipt of a valid and complete DMCA takedown notice, MathHub Pro’s administration will take the following actions:

  • We will review the claim to ensure all required legal elements are present.
  • If valid, we will expeditiously remove or disable access to the infringing material from our servers and web directories.
  • We will notify the user or content provider who uploaded or linked the allegedly infringing material (if applicable) that the material has been removed due to a copyright claim.

5. Counter-Notice Procedure

If you believe that your content was removed (or to which access was disabled) by mistake or misidentification, you have the right to file a counter-notice. To be effective, a counter-notice must be a written communication sent to our email address (mathhubpro@gmail.com) that includes substantially the following:

  • Your physical or electronic signature.
  • Identification of the material that has been removed and the specific URL location at which the material appeared before it was removed.
  • A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good faith belief that the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification of the material to be removed or disabled.
  • Your name, address, and telephone number, and a statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the courts located in Telangana, India, and that you will accept service of process from the person who provided the original takedown notification.

6. Repeat Infringer Policy

In accordance with the DMCA and other applicable laws, MathHub Pro has adopted a policy of terminating, in appropriate circumstances and at our sole discretion, the accounts of registered users who are deemed to be repeat infringers. We may also, at our sole discretion, limit access to the site and/or terminate the accounts of any users who infringe any intellectual property rights of others, whether or not there is any repeat infringement.
